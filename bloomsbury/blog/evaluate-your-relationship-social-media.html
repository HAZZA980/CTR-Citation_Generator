Evaluate your relationship with social media

By Stella Cottrell

Part of Mental Health Awareness Week 2023: 15th to 21st May

Go to the Mental health collection

How much do you think about...?

  • What you see or hear on social media?
  • What is being said about you on social media?
  • The number of followers, likes, etc. that you get on social media?
  • Missing out if you don’t keep checking social media?

Social media can add to stress in many ways, from feelings of inadequacy about personal popularity to bullying or being shamed. FOMO – or ‘fear of missing out’ – drives high levels of anxiety, and for some people this involves checking social media every few minutes.

If you are preoccupied by what others think and do via social media, or find that social media add to your stress, think again about your usage. You don’t need to give social media up altogether if you don’t want to, but you can change the way you use them so that they become useful and fun again.

If you don’t use social media much, or at all, you may wish to consider the tips and activity below in relation to your use of email or news channels.

Moving forward, think about...

When you use social media

Look closely at when you check social media. Do you:

  • Check social media as soon as you wake up?
  • Check social media many times during the day?
  • Flick through social media when you are doing other things or in the middle of conversations?
  • Start to feel anxious if you haven’t checked your social media?
  • Find your use of social media is eating into your day, preventing you from getting as much done as you would like?

If you said ‘yes’ to one or more of these, you might be becoming over-reliant on social media.

How you feel when using social media

How do you feel when you’re on social media? Happy? Overwhelmed? Excited? Confused? Something else?

If social media are improving your mood and bringing you pleasure and good connections, that is great. If you feel your engagement with social media ultimately brings you down, leaving you disappointed or anxious, maybe it’s time for a rethink.

That might mean changing which platforms you are using, your connections, what you use social media for – or how much you use them at all!

What you use it for

Observe how much genuinely useful information you pick up via social media and whose posts bring you enjoyment. Focus more on these. Cut out the dross.

Look at how much time spent on social media is useful to you. Consider where you could save time for other things.

Why you use it

Bring greater awareness to why you are using social media. Is it to:

  • Keep in touch with friends and family?
  • Coordinate group projects?
  • Find new recipes?
  • Receive updates about local events?
  • Follow the news?

Consider whether it really fulfils your objectives or whether there might be better ways of achieving them.

Review the different approaches to managing how you interact with social media below. Select any that you feel would be helpful to you to add them to a list of 'Favourites', which you can then save to your journal.
